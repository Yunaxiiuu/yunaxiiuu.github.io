English The Great Gatsby Chapter 7 Summary

Gatsby, consumed by his love for Daisy, cancels his parties and hires shady servants to avoid gossip. On a hot summer day, Nick visits Tom and Daisy's home, where he finds Gatsby and Jordan Baker. Gatsby is stunned to see Daisy's baby, while Daisy seems indifferent. Their feelings for each other are palpable, leading Daisy to suggest a trip to the city. Tom, eager for confrontation, drives them to New York. At Wilson’s garage, they learn Myrtle has discovered her husband’s infidelity and plans to move west. In New York, Tom confronts Gatsby about his past and accuses him of bootlegging. As tensions rise, Daisy, initially in love with Gatsby, feels drawn back to Tom. After the heated exchange, Nick realizes it’s his thirtieth birthday. On the way back, they discover Myrtle has been fatally hit by a car, likely Gatsby’s yellow automobile. Gatsby admits to Nick that Daisy was driving but insists he will take the blame. Meanwhile, Nick finds Tom and Daisy reconciled, leaving Gatsby alone in the moonlight.

Lakas ng Pamilya Sa buhay na ito'y may dusa at pait, Ngunit sa pamilya, lungkot ay humupa. Sila'y kanlungan ko sa hirap, ginhawa, Pagmamahal nila’y di kayang mawala. Kahit anong hirap, kahit anong gulo, Sa kanilang piling, ako'y laging buo, Pamilya ang sagot sa bawat pagsubok, Kasama ko sila, sa lahat ng sulok. Sa bawat ngiti't hikbi ng aking buhay, Pamilya ang dahilan, kaya't matibay, Pagmamahal sa kanila'y aking alay, Mga pangarap ko, para sa kanila.

Economics Reflection for lesson 1

(Fundamental economic problems) These problems revolve around three main question: what to produce, how to produce, and for whom to produce. What to produce focuses on essentials, especially during emergencies when basic needs like food are prioritized. How to produce examine's methods, with a growing emphasis on sustainable practices to protect the environment. For whom to produce highlights issues of fairness, particularly regarding with wealth disparities.
